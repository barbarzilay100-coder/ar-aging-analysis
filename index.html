Accounts receivable aging & collections
How it works & assumptions

What this is. A live accounts-receivable aging console over a synthetic invoice ledger. Every figure on this page is a real SQL query executed in your browser (SQLite compiled to WebAssembly) — nothing is hard-coded.

Tabs. Dashboard — portfolio KPIs, trends and receivables aging. Collections Watchlist — rule-based risk flags on the live ledger. AI Extract — parse a pasted invoice and cross-check it against the ledger. SQL Console — run your own queries.

Data & currency. Deterministic synthetic data — 30 customers, 180 invoices, fixed seed, dates generated relative to today so the book always looks current. All amounts are shown in ILS (₪). Invoices issued in another currency are converted to ILS using the latest published exchange rate, fetched live when the page loads (with a fixed fallback if the rate service is unavailable). A small currency tag marks any invoice converted from a non-ILS original.

Metric definitions.

  • DSO (90-day) — standard simple-period Days Sales Outstanding: gross open receivables (invoice value of unpaid financed invoices) ÷ total invoiced volume of the trailing 90 days × 90.
  • Avg days to collect — average days an invoice stays open, from issue date to repayment (or to today if still unpaid). Unlike DSO this is a per-invoice average, unweighted by amount — the two answer different questions, so both are shown.
  • Open exposure — total outstanding advances on open invoices (status Financed or Overdue).
  • At-risk exposure — the outstanding advance of the distinct invoices flagged on the Watchlist. It is a subset of Open exposure, and it differs from the sum of the per-rule "Rule impact" amounts because one invoice can trip several rules, where each impact is counted separately.
